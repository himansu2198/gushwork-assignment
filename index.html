Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our HDPE pipes, ordering process, and technical support.

HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) pipe is a thermoplastic piping solution known for its high strength-to-density ratio. Key advantages include exceptional corrosion resistance, leak-proof heat-fused joints, flexibility for trenchless installation, a service life exceeding 50 years, and suitability for potable water, gas, and industrial applications. It is also fully recyclable, making it an environmentally responsible choice.

We manufacture HDPE pipes from 20mm to 1500mm in diameter, covering pressure ratings from PN 2.5 to PN 16 (2.5 to 16 bar). Standard Dimension Ratios (SDR) range from SDR 11 to SDR 33. Custom sizes and pressure classes can be produced upon request for specific project requirements.

HDPE pipes are commonly joined using three methods: Butt Fusion (heat-welding pipe ends together for a seamless monolithic joint), Electrofusion (using fittings with embedded electrical resistance elements), and Mechanical couplings for above-ground or repair applications. All fusion methods produce joints as strong as or stronger than the pipe itself, eliminating potential leak points.

Our pipes are certified to IS 4984 (Indian Standard), ISO 4427 (international water supply standard), ASTM D3035, and DIN 8074/8075 (German standards). We hold BIS certification and our manufacturing facility is ISO 9001:2015 certified. CE marking is available for products destined for European markets.

Minimum order quantities vary by pipe size and type. Standard sizes are typically available ex-stock with shipping in 6–12 business days. For custom specifications, project-specific orders, or large infrastructure contracts, lead times are 3–6 weeks depending on volume. Contact our sales team for a detailed quote and delivery schedule tailored to your project timeline.

Advanced HDPE Pipe Manufacturing Process

Every pipe we produce follows a rigorous, multi-stage manufacturing workflow — ensuring consistent quality, dimensional accuracy, and long-term field performance.

High-Grade Raw Material Selection

We source only certified PE 100 grade HDPE compounds from approved suppliers, tested for melt flow index and density before entering production. Every batch is traceable from raw resin to finished pipe.

  • PE 100 grade compound with MFI testing on every batch
  • UV stabilisers and carbon black added for outdoor durability
  • Approved supplier chain with full material traceability

Precision Screw Extrusion

HDPE compound is fed into a high-torque single-screw extruder, where it is heated and plasticised under controlled temperature zones before being forced through a precision die to form the pipe profile.

  • Temperature-zoned barrel for consistent melt quality
  • Precision-machined dies for tight wall thickness tolerance
  • Continuous melt pressure monitoring throughout run

Controlled Water Tank Cooling

Freshly extruded pipe passes through a series of calibrated water cooling tanks. Carefully controlled water temperature and spray patterns ensure uniform solidification without internal stress or warping.

  • Multi-stage cooling tanks with temperature control
  • Prevents residual stress and out-of-round deformation
  • Cooling rate matched to pipe wall thickness and diameter

Dimensional Sizing & Calibration

Pipe outer diameter and wall thickness are continuously measured using laser gauges and vacuum sizing sleeves. Any deviation triggers an automatic alarm and line adjustment to maintain specification compliance.

  • Laser OD measurement at ±0.1 mm accuracy
  • Vacuum sizing sleeve locks outer diameter to spec
  • Real-time feedback loop for automatic correction

Rigorous In-Line Quality Control

Every pipe is subjected to hydrostatic pressure testing, visual inspection, and dimensional checks before leaving the production line. Finished pipes are batch-sampled for tensile, impact, and oxidative induction time (OIT) testing.

  • 100% hydrostatic pressure test to 1.5× rated pressure
  • Tensile and notch-impact testing per ISO 6259
  • OIT testing confirms long-term oxidative resistance

Permanent Pipe Identification Marking

Each pipe is ink-jet printed at regular intervals with all mandatory identification: manufacturer name, product standard, size, SDR, pressure rating, material grade, and production date code — fully compliant with BIS and ISO marking requirements.

  • Marking every 1 m per IS 4984 / ISO 4427 requirements
  • Includes diameter, SDR, PN rating, and batch code
  • UV-resistant ink that remains legible over pipe lifetime

Precision Automatic Cut-to-Length

Pipes are cut to specified lengths using travelling saw cutters synchronised with line speed, producing clean, square ends ready for immediate fusion jointing. Standard lengths are 6 m and 12 m; custom lengths can be specified.

  • Planetary saw cutter for burr-free, square end cuts
  • Encoder-synchronised with haul-off for length accuracy
  • Custom cut lengths available on project orders

Safe Bundling & Dispatch Packaging

Cut pipes are bundled, strapped, and labelled for safe transport and storage. Coils are wound to standard diameters and wrapped in protective film. Every bundle or coil carries a full identification label with QR code for digital traceability.

  • Pipes bundled in hexagonal packs for transport stability
  • Coils film-wrapped to protect from UV and contamination
  • QR-coded labels for complete digital batch traceability
